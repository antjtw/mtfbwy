Chapter 4

Choose a Career

Your career shapes your starting gear, special features, and proficiencies — the livelihood that defines who you are in the galaxy's economy of survival.

💡
Expertise on overlap

If you gain a proficiency from your career that you already have from your Species, Origin, or Path, you instead gain Expertise in that skill.

Bounty Hunter

Core Rules

Feared across the galaxy, you are a predator of sentients — capturing, hunting, or eliminating for credits.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, an Advanced survival pack, and Torturer's tools ProficienciesSurvival (Expertise if already proficient) and Torturer's tools
Feared

You have Advantage on Coercion rolls.

Resourceful Hunter

Make a casting roll against a target to ensnare them in an energy snare. On a success, mark off 1 Willpower slot and immobilise your target. Your casting modifier is your Intelligence (INT) modifier + proficiency bonus.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Freedom Fighter

Core Rules

You've fought against oppression, tyranny, and corruption — whether for your own people or for the galaxy itself.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, an Advanced survival pack, and Armourer's tools ProficienciesSurvival (Expertise if already proficient) and Armourer's tools
Bolstered Resolve

Once per Full Rest, when you are reduced to 1 Willpower slot, you regain Willpower slots equal to your Presence (PRE) modifier.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Jedi Knight

Core Rules

A servant of the Order, trained in the ancient ways of the Jedi. Depending on your level, you may also be a Padawan or Master.

Starting EquipmentAny kyber weapon, Jedi robes (no protection, +2 Evasion), a Devotee's pack, and Academic's tools ProficienciesReligion (Expertise if already proficient) and Academic's tools
Trained in the Jedi Arts

Gain access to Basic Force Powers. You may select an additional Tier 0 Force Power if you gain this feature from your Origin or Path.

Last updated 18 Feb 2026 | Closed Beta v1.0

Pilot

Core Rules

From small freighters to starfighters, you know how to fly — and how to survive long enough to fly again.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, a Spacer's pack, and Cartographer's tools ProficienciesPiloting (Expertise if already proficient) and Cartographer's tools
Skilled Pilot

Add your Agility (AGI) modifier to your ship's Evasion during space combat.

Port Familiar

You know someone in nearly every starport. Once per Full Rest, when you arrive somewhere new, you may declare a contact you know there — a mechanic, a smuggler, a dock officer, an old friend. The GM works with you on the details. Treat them as a Friendly NPC for one favour, after which their attitude is up to the GM.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Scholar

Core Rules

Knowledge is your weapon. From academia to fieldwork, you've spent your life in pursuit of understanding.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, an Advanced survival pack, and Academic's tools ProficienciesHistory (Expertise if already proficient) and Academic's tools
Sage Advice

Offer advice to a number of allies who can hear you equal to your Intelligence (INT) modifier. Select a skill or tool you are proficient with. Once in the next hour, the next time an advised ally makes a roll using the chosen skill or tool, they may add your proficiency bonus to the roll if they are not already proficient.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Smuggler

Core Rules

You thrive in the shadows of law and empire — dealing in secrets, contraband, and quick escapes.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, common clothes, a Spacer's pack, and Thieves' tools ProficienciesSkullduggery (Expertise if already proficient) and Thieves' tools
Hidden Compartments

Advantage on Stealth or Skullduggery rolls when concealing items.

Deceptive Charm

Once per Full Rest, you may reroll a failed Skullduggery roll and take the higher result.

Expanded Careers

📖
Sourcebook content

These careers are drawn from supplemental sourcebooks. Confirm with your GM whether expanded content is available at your table.

Criminal

Galactic Occupations

You came up inside the system — the other system. Gangs, syndicates, smuggling rings, the underworld networks that move credits and contraband through every starport and slum.

Starting EquipmentAny blaster pistol or melee weapon, a Streetwise pack, and Thieves' tools ProficienciesSkullduggery (Expertise if already proficient) and Thieves' tools
Connected

You have ties to a criminal organisation — gang, syndicate, or local crew, decided with the GM at character creation. Once per session, you may declare you know someone here — a fence, an enforcer, a contact who owes a favour. The GM works with you on the details. Treat them as a known figure within the underworld, with their own loyalties and demands.

Reading the Room

Advantage on Insight rolls to detect lies, threats, or hidden weapons in social situations, and on Perception rolls to notice surveillance, marks, or tails.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

DJ

Galactic Occupations

Not all heroes carry a blaster — some carry a beat. A DJ controls the mood, the rhythm, and the energy of a crowd.

Starting EquipmentAny standard blaster, a Streetwise pack, a portable sound rig, and Slicer's tools ProficienciesCoordination (Expertise if already proficient) and Slicer's tools
Adept Controller

Advantage on Technology or Coordination rolls made to operate, override, or improvise with physical interfaces — instrument panels, control rigs, instruments, vehicle controls.

Read the Room

Once per Full Rest, declare what the dominant mood is (fearful, hostile, eager, distracted, sympathetic) and the GM confirms or corrects you. Gain Advantage on your next Influence roll that plays into that mood.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Droidsmith

Allies & Adversaries

Specialist in creation, repair, and modification. You know droids inside and out — to you, machines are companions, puzzles, and weapons alike.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, a Tradesperson's pack, and Droidsmith's tools ProficienciesTechnology (Expertise if already proficient) and Droidsmith's tools
Droid Saviour

Mark off 1 Willpower slot to repair any droid within Close range by 1 Hit Point slot.

Droid Speaker

Advantage on Negotiation rolls with droids and cybernetic beings. You can also understand the binary, beeps, and chitters of droids without a translator.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Force Scholar

Luthen Rael's Galactic Antiquities

You live for the mysteries of the Force — studying its past, preserving its secrets, and unearthing ancient truths.

Starting EquipmentAny blaster, scholar's robes (no protection, +2 Evasion), a Devotee's pack, and Academic's tools ProficienciesReligion or History (Expertise if already proficient) and Academic's tools
Otherworldly Knowledge

Advantage on Religion and History rolls relating to the Force, ancient orders, and Force-using cultures.

Echoes in the Dust

When you spend time at a Force-touched site — a ruin, a relic, a place where the Force has gathered — you may make a History or Religion roll to glimpse what happened there. The GM tells you something true about its past on a success.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Gladiator

Galactic Occupations

Raised in the pits, gladiators are brutal fighters who turn combat into spectacle.

Starting EquipmentAny melee physical weapon, a shield or armour, a Streetwise pack, and Armourer's tools ProficienciesCoercion (Expertise if already proficient) and Armourer's tools
Blood and Sand

When you reduce an adversary to 0 Hit Point slots with a Minor Critical Success, regain 4 Willpower slots (up to your maximum) as adrenaline and the roar of the crowd surge through you.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Jedi Wayseeker

Beyond the Stormwall

Unlike other Jedi, you follow no council — only the Force itself. You travel freely, guided by instinct and purpose.

Starting EquipmentAny kyber weapon, Jedi robes (no protection, +2 Evasion), comm link, an Advanced survival pack, and Cartographer's tools ProficienciesSurvival (Expertise if already proficient) and Cartographer's tools
Trained in the Jedi Arts

Gain access to Basic Force Powers. You may select an additional Tier 0 Force Power if you gain this feature from your Origin or Path.

Guided by the Force

Advantage on Survival rolls whilst travelling unfamiliar terrain. Once per Full Rest, when you would fail an Insight or Survival roll whilst seeking a path, person, or truth, you may reroll and take the higher result.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Labourer

Galactic Occupations

Your strength and endurance were forged through toil in fields, factories, or mines.

Starting EquipmentAny standard weapon, work clothes, a Tradesperson's pack, and a toolkit of your choice — Armourer's tools, Mechanic's tools, or Herbalist's tools ProficienciesAthletics (Expertise if already proficient) and your chosen toolkit
Grit

You gain +1 Hit Point slot (starting maximum 7 instead of 6).

Hard Graft

When working on a physical task that requires sustained effort over time — repairs, hauling, digging, building — you complete the work in half the time it would take an unskilled hand.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Meknek

Galactic Occupations

Hull-crawlers and vacuum-walkers — mechanics who risk their lives repairing ships mid-flight or in deep space.

Starting EquipmentAny armour, work clothes, gripton boots, a Spacer's pack, and Mechanic's tools ProficienciesAthletics (Expertise if already proficient) and Mechanic's tools
Risk Averse

Whilst in zero gravity, you ignore Disadvantage on all rolls. Advantage on saving rolls made to avoid falling, slipping, or being thrown clear of a surface.

Hull Sense

When making a Technology roll to repair, diagnose, or jury-rig a starship, you may add your Resilience (RES) modifier to the roll instead of Intelligence.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Outlaw

Galactic Occupations

You're wanted across systems — for rebellion, for refusal, for crimes the powerful would rather forget.

Starting EquipmentAny blaster, light armour, an Advanced survival pack, a forged identichip or false papers, and Thieves' tools ProficienciesStealth (Expertise if already proficient) and Thieves' tools
Wanted

You begin play with a bounty on your head — GM and you decide who placed it, why, and how big. When the bounty is active in a scene, Advantage on Stealth rolls to move unseen, and on Insight rolls to spot informers and tails.

Nothing Left to Lose

Once per Full Rest, when you would mark off your last Willpower slot, instead clear 1 Willpower slot and gain Advantage on your next action roll.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Treasure Hunter

Galactic Occupations

Explorer of ruins, raider of tombs, finder of lost riches — fortune and glory are your goals.

Starting EquipmentAny blaster pistol or physical weapon, light armour, an Advanced survival pack, and Cartographer's tools ProficienciesPerception (Expertise if already proficient) and Cartographer's tools
Fortune Favours the Bold

Once per encounter, reroll a failed Agility or Perception roll and take the higher result.

Lore of the Lost

When you encounter ruins, ancient sites, or relics of a forgotten civilisation, make a History roll to recall a useful detail. The GM tells you something true and useful on a success.

Last updated 5 May 2026 | Closed Beta v1.1

Starter Packs

Each career lists a starting pack. You receive the full contents at character creation.

Basic Survival Pack

15 ration portions, 3 healing patches, communicator, bedroll, purifying canteen, glowrod, fibrecord (6m), multi-tool

Advanced Survival Pack

20 ration portions, 5 healing stims, long-range communicator, bedroll, purifying canteen, glowrod, fire starters, macrobinoculars

Spacer's Pack

15 ration portions, 3 healing stims, communicator, bedroll, canteen, glowrod, 6 power cells, fibrecord (6m) with mag-clamps

Devotee's Pack

10 ration portions, 3 healing patches, communicator, bedroll, canteen, glow lamp, portable holo-projector, field stabiliser (1 charge)

Tradesperson's Pack

10 ration portions, 3 healing patches, communicator, bedroll, canteen, glowrod, spare parts pouch, hand-held diagnostic scanner

Streetwise Pack

10 ration portions, 3 healing patches, communicator, bedroll, canteen, glowrod, datapad, lockspike (one-use)
