Origins & Careers

Where you came from shapes who you are. Your origin represents your earliest steps into a larger world. Your career is what you've made of yourself since.

💡
Expertise on Overlap

If you gain a proficiency in a skill from your career that you already have from your Species, Origin, or Path, you instead gain Expertise in that skill.

Origins

Every Hero has an origin. Each grants you a unique feature or ability, a trait modifier increase, and a tool or skill proficiency. These were your first steps — they cannot be changed later.

Abandoned

Core Rules

Left behind to fend for yourself, you learned to survive where others failed.

Trait IncreaseResilience (RES) +1 ProficiencySurvival
Survivor's Instinct

Once per short rest, reroll a failed Survival or Perception check. You must use the new roll.

Careers

If you gain a proficiency in a skill from your career that you have already obtained from your Species, Origin or Path, you instead gain Expertise in that skill.

Bounty Hunter

Core Rules

Feared across the galaxy, you are a predator of sentients — capturing, hunting, or eliminating for credits.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, an Advanced survival pack, and Torturer's tools ProficiencySurvival (Expertise if already proficient) and Torturer's tools
Feared

You have Advantage on Coercion rolls.

Resourceful Hunter

Make a casting roll against a target to ensnare them in an energy snare. On a success, mark off 1 Willpower slot and immobilise your target. Your casting modifier is your Intelligence modifier + proficiency bonus.

Freedom Fighter

Core Rules

You've fought against oppression, tyranny, and corruption — whether for your own people or for the galaxy itself.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, an Advanced survival pack, and Armourer's tools ProficiencySurvival (Expertise if already proficient) and Armourer's tools
Bolstered Resolve

Once per Full Rest, when you are reduced to 1 Willpower slot, you regain Willpower slots equal to your Presence (PRE) modifier.

Jedi Knight

Core Rules

A servant of the Order, trained in the ancient ways of the Jedi. Depending on your level, you may also be a Padawan or Master.

Starting EquipmentAny kyber weapon, Jedi robes (no protection, +2 to Evasion), a Devotee's pack, and Academic's tools ProficiencyReligion (Expertise if already proficient) and Academic's tools
Trained in the Jedi Arts

Gain access to Basic Force Powers. You may select an additional Tier 0 Force Power if you gain this feature from your Origin or Path.

Pilot

Core Rules

From small freighters to starfighters, you know how to fly — and how to survive long enough to fly again.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, a Spacer's pack, and Cartographer's tools ProficiencyPiloting (Expertise if already proficient) and Cartographer's tools
Skilled Pilot

Add your Agility (AGI) modifier to your ship's Evasion during space combat.

Port Familiar

You know someone in nearly every starport. Once per Full Rest, when you arrive somewhere new, you may declare a contact you know there — a mechanic, a smuggler, a dock officer, an old friend. The GM works with you on the details. Treat them as a Friendly NPC for one favour, after which their attitude is up to the GM.

Scholar

Core Rules

Knowledge is your weapon. From academia to fieldwork, you've spent your life in pursuit of understanding.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, an Advanced survival pack, and Academic's tools ProficiencyHistory (Expertise if already proficient) and Academic's tools
Sage Advice

Offer advice to a number of allies who can hear you equal to your Intelligence modifier. Select a skill or tool you are proficient with. Once in the next hour, the next time an advised ally makes a roll using the chosen skill or tool, they may add your proficiency bonus to the roll if they are not already proficient.

Smuggler

Core Rules

You thrive in the shadows of law and empire — dealing in secrets, contraband, and quick escapes.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, common clothes, a Spacer's pack, and Thieves' tools ProficiencySkullduggery (Expertise if already proficient) and Thieves' tools
Hidden Compartments

Advantage on Stealth or Skullduggery rolls when concealing items.

Deceptive Charm

Once per Full Rest, you may reroll a failed Skullduggery roll and take the higher result.

Extended Careers

These careers are available from supplemental sourcebooks. Confirm availability with your GM.

Criminal

You came up inside the system — the other system. Gangs, syndicates, smuggling rings, the underworld networks that move credits and contraband through every starport and slum.

Starting EquipmentAny blaster pistol or melee weapon, a Streetwise pack, and Thieves' tools ProficiencySkullduggery (Expertise if already proficient) and Thieves' tools
Connected

You have ties to a criminal organisation — gang, syndicate, or local crew — decided with the GM at character creation. Once per session, you may declare you know someone here — a fence, an enforcer, a contact who owes a favour.

Reading the Room

Advantage on Insight rolls to detect lies, threats, or hidden weapons in social situations. Advantage on Perception rolls to notice surveillance, marks, or tails.

DJ

Not all heroes carry a blaster — some carry a beat. A DJ controls the mood, the rhythm, and the energy of a crowd.

Starting EquipmentAny standard blaster, a Streetwise pack, a portable sound rig, and Slicer's tools ProficiencyCoordination (Expertise if already proficient) and Slicer's tools
Adept Controller

Advantage on Technology or Coordination rolls made to operate, override, or improvise with physical interfaces — instrument panels, control rigs, vehicle controls.

Read the Room

Once per Full Rest, declare what the dominant mood of a crowd, cantina, or room is (fearful, hostile, eager, distracted, sympathetic). The GM confirms or corrects you. You then gain Advantage on your next Influence roll that plays into that mood.

Droidsmith

Specialist in creation, repair, and modification. You know droids inside and out — to you, machines are companions, puzzles, and weapons alike.

Starting EquipmentAny weapon and armour, a Tradesperson's pack, and Droidsmith's tools ProficiencyTechnology (Expertise if already proficient) and Droidsmith's tools
Droid Saviour

Mark off 1 Willpower slot to repair any droid within Close range by 1 Hit Point slot.

Droid Speaker

Advantage on Negotiation rolls with droids and cybernetic beings. You can also understand the binary, beeps, and chitters of droids without a translator.

Force Scholar

You live for the mysteries of the Force — studying its past, preserving its secrets, and unearthing ancient truths.

Starting EquipmentAny blaster, scholar's robes (no protection, +2 Evasion), a Devotee's pack, and Academic's tools ProficiencyReligion or History (Expertise if already proficient) and Academic's tools
Otherworldly Knowledge

Advantage on Religion and History rolls relating to the Force, ancient orders, and Force-using cultures.

Echoes in the Dust

When you spend time at a Force-touched site — a ruin, a relic, a place where the Force has gathered — you may make a History or Religion roll to glimpse what happened there. The GM tells you something true about its past on a success.

Gladiator

Raised in the pits, gladiators are brutal fighters who turn combat into spectacle.

Starting EquipmentAny melee physical weapon, a shield or armour, a Streetwise pack, and Armourer's tools ProficiencyCoercion (Expertise if already proficient) and Armourer's tools
Blood and Sand

When you reduce an adversary to 0 Hit Point slots with a Minor Critical Success, regain 4 Willpower slots (up to your maximum) as adrenaline and the roar of the crowd surge through you.

Jedi Wayseeker

Unlike other Jedi, you follow no council — only the Force itself. You travel freely, guided by instinct and purpose.

Starting EquipmentAny kyber weapon, Jedi robes (no protection, +2 Evasion), comm link, an Advanced survival pack, and Cartographer's tools ProficiencySurvival (Expertise if already proficient) and Cartographer's tools
Trained in the Jedi Arts

Gain access to Basic Force Powers. You may select an additional Tier 0 Force Power if you gain this feature from your Origin or Path.

Guided by the Force

Advantage on Survival rolls whilst travelling unfamiliar terrain or making your way to an unvisited destination. Once per Full Rest, when you would fail an Insight or Survival roll whilst seeking a path, person, or truth, you may reroll and take the higher result.

Labourer

Your strength and endurance were forged through toil in fields, factories, or mines.

Starting EquipmentAny standard weapon, work clothes, a Tradesperson's pack, and a toolkit of your choice (Armourer's, Mechanic's, or Herbalist's tools) ProficiencyAthletics (Expertise if already proficient) and your chosen toolkit
Grit

You gain +1 Hit Point slot (starting maximum 7 instead of 6).

Hard Graft

When working on a physical task that requires sustained effort over time — repairs, hauling, digging, building — you complete the work in half the time it would take an unskilled hand.

Meknek

Hull-crawlers and vacuum-walkers, mekneks are mechanics who risk their lives repairing ships mid-flight or in deep space.

Starting EquipmentAny armour, work clothes, gripton boots, a Spacer's pack, and Mechanic's tools ProficiencyAthletics (Expertise if already proficient) and Mechanic's tools
Risk Averse

While in zero gravity, you ignore Disadvantage on all rolls — gripton boots and hard-earned instinct keep you anchored. Advantage on saving rolls made to avoid falling, slipping, or being thrown clear of a surface.

Hull Sense

When making a Technology roll to repair, diagnose, or jury-rig a starship — whether dockside or mid-flight — you may add your Resilience (RES) modifier to the roll instead of Intelligence.

Outlaw

You're wanted across systems — for rebellion, for refusal, for crimes the powerful would rather forget. You ride alone or with the few who'd never sell you out.

Starting EquipmentAny blaster, light armour, an Advanced survival pack, a forged identichip or false papers, and Thieves' tools ProficiencyStealth (Expertise if already proficient) and Thieves' tools
Wanted

You begin play with a bounty on your head — the GM and you decide together who placed it, why, and how big it is. When the bounty is active in a scene, you have Advantage on Stealth rolls to move unseen and on Insight rolls to spot informers and tails.

Nothing Left to Lose

Once per Full Rest, when you would mark off your last Willpower slot, you instead clear 1 Willpower slot and gain Advantage on your next action roll.

Treasure Hunter

Explorer of ruins, raider of tombs, finder of lost riches — fortune and glory are your goals.

Starting EquipmentAny blaster pistol or physical weapon, light armour, an Advanced survival pack, and Cartographer's tools ProficiencyPerception (Expertise if already proficient) and Cartographer's tools
Fortune Favours the Bold

Once per encounter, reroll a failed Agility or Perception roll and take the higher result.

Lore of the Lost

When you encounter ruins, ancient sites, or relics of a forgotten civilisation, make a History roll to recall a useful detail — a layout, a hazard, a likely hiding place. The GM tells you something true and useful on a success.

Starter Packs

Each career provides a curated bundle of items designed to reflect that career's training, background, and expected mission loadout. You receive the full contents of your pack during character creation.

Basic Survival Pack

15 ration portions (dry travel biscuits), 3 healing patches, communicator, bedroll, purifying canteen, glowrod, fibrecord (6m), multi-tool

Advanced Survival Pack

20 ration portions (field rations), 5 healing stims, long-range communicator, bedroll, purifying canteen, glowrod, fire starters, macrobinoculars

Spacer's Pack

15 ration portions (compressed ship rations), 3 healing stims, communicator, bedroll, canteen, glowrod, 6 power cells, fibrecord (6m) with mag-clamps

Devotee's Pack

10 ration portions (fasting wafers), 3 healing patches, communicator, bedroll, purifying canteen, glow lamp, portable holo-projector, field stabiliser (1 charge)

Tradesperson's Pack

10 ration portions (street food and preserves), 3 healing patches, communicator, bedroll, canteen, glowrod, spare parts pouch, hand-held diagnostic scanner

Streetwise Pack

10 ration portions (street food and dried fruit), 3 healing patches, communicator, bedroll, canteen, glowrod, datapad, lockspike (one-use)
