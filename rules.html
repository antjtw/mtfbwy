Core Rules

MTFBYW is a game of Willpower and fate. The GM describes scenarios from a galaxy far, far away. Players share the Focus to describe how their Heroes react and what they do next.

Gameplay Loop

The core gameplay loop is the rhythm that drives every scene — whether in the heat of combat or the quiet tension between it.

  1. Set the Scene

    The GM paints the moment — describing the Heroes' surroundings and bringing the world into focus. Its dangers, its people, and the details that matter. This is where stakes begin to emerge.

  2. Ask and Answer Questions

    The Heroes look closer, asking questions to better understand the situation. The GM answers with what can be readily known, or turns the question back to the table to deepen the moment.

  3. Build the Story

    As the scene unfolds, opportunities arise. The Heroes describe what they do and how they act. If success is certain, the story moves forward without a roll. When the outcome is uncertain, the dice are called upon.

  4. Return to the Scene

    The cycle continues. The GM reframes the moment, reflecting the consequences of the Heroes' actions and revealing what has changed.

Focus

When a Hero, NPC, or Adversary is acting, they are in Focus — the immediate centre of both the narrative and the game mechanics. Focus is passed around the table continuously, and in some cases, even the environment can hold it.

⚖️
No Initiative, No Fixed Turn Order

The flow of action follows the Focus. When it's yours, it's your moment — to express who your Hero is through roleplay, or to test what they're made of through an action roll. When that moment resolves, Focus shifts to someone else.

How Focus Moves

Typically, Focus moves to someone the story naturally follows next, someone who hasn't had Focus in a while, or someone chosen by a triggered mechanic.

  • In social settings, Focus passes organically between Heroes, NPCs, and non-aggressive Adversaries without formal declaration
  • Once the Dyad Dice begin to roll, Focus remains with the Heroes until they fail a roll — then it swings to the GM, who may make a GM move
  • The GM can seize Focus at any moment by spending Despair, and may continue spending Despair to shift it between Adversaries before returning it to the Heroes
  • A Hero can spend Willpower to pull Focus back

Balance & Despair

Make any action roll using your Dyad Dice — two d12s, one for Balance and one for Despair (different colours). You do not expend or gain anything before the roll.

What Are You Rolling Against?

Every roll has a number to beat — the Difficulty. Meet or exceed it, and you succeed. Fall short, and you don't. Difficulty comes from:

  • An Adversary: Attack rolls aim for their Evasion. Most other rolls aim for their Difficulty score.
  • The Situation: The GM sets the Difficulty. A good rule of thumb is 12 + the relevant modifier.
  • An Extended Challenge: Some tasks use a Target Score — a running total you build toward over several rolls.

The 7 Roll Outcomes

✨ Success with Balance
Total meets or beats Difficulty. Balance Die shows higher result. You succeed and clear 1 Willpower slot.
⚡ Success with Despair
Total meets or beats Difficulty. Despair Die shows higher result. You succeed with a cost or complication. You do not clear Willpower. GM gains a Despair or makes a free move.
🔸 Failure with Balance
Total falls short. Balance Die shows higher result. You fail with a minor consequence but still clear 1 Willpower slot. Focus immediately shifts to the GM.
💥 Failure with Despair
Total falls short. Despair Die shows higher result. You fail with a major consequence, mark off a Willpower slot, and the GM gains a Despair. Focus immediately shifts to the GM.
🎯 Minor Critical Success
Both Dyad Dice show matching results (2–11). You automatically succeed regardless of the total. If an attack roll, roll all damage dice twice and add modifiers once. Clear 3 Willpower slots. Counts as a roll with Balance.
⭐ Major Critical Success
Both Dyad Dice show 12. You automatically succeed. If an attack roll, take maximum possible damage dice result, multiply by 2, then add modifiers once. Clear all Willpower slots. Counts as Balance.
💀 Major Critical Failure
Both Dyad Dice show 1. You automatically fail. Roll on the Critical Failure table. Mark 3 Willpower slots. GM gains 3 Despair. Focus shifts to the GM. Counts as Despair.

Target Score

Some challenges are too big to settle with a single roll — repairing a crashed speeder, slicing through a fortress of ice, researching a forgotten ritual. These are resolved using a Target Score.

A Target Score is written as Target / Difficulty — for example, 48/12.

  • The Target is the total progress you must reach to complete the task
  • The Difficulty is the number each individual roll must meet or beat to count as a success
🔧
The Right Tool

Many Target Scores call for the right tool or kit. Attempting the work without one is possible, but rarely wise. The right gear speeds the work; the wrong gear — or none at all — makes every roll harder.

Roll ResultProgress Added
Major Critical SuccessInstant completion
Minor Critical SuccessDice total, doubled
Success with BalanceFull dice total
Success with DespairFull dice total
Failure with BalanceFull dice total
Failure with DespairNo progress
Major Critical FailureNo progress (GM may erase prior progress)

A successful roll always adds progress — cleanly on Balance, or at a cost on Despair. Even a failed roll can count, so long as Balance is with you. A failed roll with Despair is wasted effort — the tool slips, the cipher resists.

Willpower

Willpower is a core resource — representing both your ability to act and your mental resilience under pressure. As it is expended, it reflects mounting strain, risk, and mental and physical expenditure. Willpower carries over between sessions.

Spending Willpower

You can spend Willpower to:

  • Activate a specific feature from your Species, Origin, Path, or Career
  • Perform a Trigger move (costs 1 Willpower per trigger)
  • Use certain Manoeuvres, such as Stand Up or Disengage
  • Help another Hero or NPC (costs 1 Willpower, then roll an advantage die)
  • Clear a Condition (where the condition allows it)
  • Interrupt the GM and seize Focus (costs 3 Willpower)

Losing Willpower

Willpower can also be depleted through Dyad Dice rolls, traversing hostile environments, or acting while Dying.

Willpower Penalties

Your first 6 Willpower slots are free to use with no penalties. Once you access the further 6 slots (available through Path progression), you incur roll penalties.

Slots MarkedRoll Penalty
1–6 slotsNo penalty
7 slots−2 on Dyad Dice rolls
8 slots−4 on Dyad Dice rolls
9 slots−6 on Dyad Dice rolls
10 slots−8 on Dyad Dice rolls
11 slots−10 on Dyad Dice rolls
All 12 slots−12 on Dyad Dice rolls and you gain the Unconscious condition (or die, if already Dying)

Restoring Willpower

  • Short rest: clear 1d4 used Willpower slots
  • Full rest: clear all Willpower slots
  • Stimulants and items may restore Willpower temporarily, but often at a cost
⚠️
Running Out of Willpower

At 0 Willpower, you gain the Unconscious condition. You remain unconscious until Willpower is restored. If you are Dying and expend your last Willpower slot, you die. If Unconscious with 0 Willpower and you expend your last Hit Point slot, you also die.

Hero Bonuses

Proficiency

Your Proficiency is determined by your level and path progression options. When you make an action roll, saving roll, or attack using something you are proficient with, add your Proficiency bonus to the roll.

  • If you have Expertise, add double your Proficiency bonus
  • If proficient with a tool or kit, add 1d6 instead of your Proficiency bonus
  • If you have Expertise with a tool or kit, add 2d6 instead

Advantage

When you have Advantage, roll an additional 1d6 and add it to your total. This does not affect Balance or Despair — only the final result.

Disadvantage

When you have Disadvantage, roll an additional 1d6 and subtract it from your total. This does not affect Balance or Despair — only the final result.

Taking Action

Every action you take, every roll you make — it all lives here. Moves, manoeuvres, triggers, and rituals are the building blocks of everything your Hero does when it counts.

Passives

Some abilities don't need activating. They're simply part of who your Hero is — baked into their training, their species, their connection to the Force, or the gear they carry.

A Passive is always in effect. It requires no action, no Willpower, and no trigger. Your Passives are listed on your character sheet and within your Path, species, and item features.

Moves (Action Rolls)

An action roll is any Dyad Dice roll made when the outcome of an action is uncertain and carries meaningful consequence.

Roll your Dyad Dice and add your attack modifier. This is usually Strength (STR) for melee weapons and Agility (AGI) for ranged weapons.

Unless stated otherwise, the Difficulty equals the target's Evasion score.

Roll your Dyad Dice and add your casting modifier, as defined by your Path or the feature itself. Unless stated otherwise, Difficulty equals the target's Difficulty score.

If a power targets multiple adversaries, make a single casting roll and a single damage roll, then apply the result to each target individually.

Make an Agility (Coordination) roll against the Adversary's Difficulty. On a success, the next time that Adversary attacks you before you take Focus again, they have Disadvantage on the attack.

You may Dodge against multiple Adversaries, making a separate roll for each.

Attempt to seize and restrain a target no more than one size larger than you. Make a Strength (Athletics) roll against the target's Difficulty.

  • On a success, the target gains the Immobilised condition
  • You may move a grappled target by dragging or lifting them — unless they are at least two sizes smaller than you, your speed is halved
  • Another Hero may contest using Agility (Coordination)

Make an Agility (Stealth) roll. While Hidden, Adversaries must rely on other senses to locate you if their passive perception is lower than your result.

  • If found, attacks against you have Advantage
  • If you attack a target that cannot see you, your attack has Advantage. Your position is revealed after the attack.

Attempt to sway an NPC or Adversary through words, presence, or intent. Describe how you approach them. The GM determines their stance:

  • Willing: Your urging aligns with the target's desires — no roll necessary
  • Unwilling: Your urging is repugnant or counter to their alignment — they don't comply without a roll
  • Hesitant: They need convincing — make an appropriate action roll. Difficulty is 12 + the target's Intelligence modifier

Make a Strength (Athletics) roll against the target's Difficulty. On a success, push the target 1.5 metres in a direction you choose or knock them Prone.

  • If the target is smaller than you: roll with Advantage
  • If the target is larger than you: roll with Disadvantage
  • You cannot shove a target more than two sizes larger than you

Make a Presence (Skulduggery) roll against the target's Difficulty. On a success, take the chosen item without alerting the target. On a failure, the attempt is noticed or leaves clear suspicion.

  • If the target is distracted or unaware: roll with Advantage
  • If the item is secured, concealed, or closely guarded: roll with Disadvantage

Take a moment to observe, recall, or analyse. Make an Intelligence roll or another relevant skill (as determined by the GM) to uncover useful information.

Manoeuvres

When a move does not require an action roll, it's known as a manoeuvre. You can make as many manoeuvres as makes sense narratively. Some require Willpower.

Disengage

Mark off 1 Willpower to disengage from Adversaries in melee engagement range. When you attempt to move away, you do not trigger an opportunity attack.

Object Interaction

You can interact with or manipulate an object in Close range — like unlocking a door with a key or picking up credits from a fallen Crime Lord.

Reload

Some weapons have ammunition or power cells that need reloading. You can reload from your inventory or within Close range.

Stand Up

If you're knocked Prone, mark off 1 Willpower to stand back to your feet.

Swap Weapon

Stowing or drawing your primary or secondary weapon is free. To use any other weapon, swap it with your primary or secondary using this manoeuvre.

Use an Item

During combat, you can use an object — from applying a med pac to splicing into a terminal with a data spike. You can also throw a non-weapon object.

Trigger Moves

When you are not in Focus, certain moments may present an opportunity to act. These are called Trigger moves.

When a Trigger occurs, you may immediately act by marking off 1 Willpower slot, unless stated otherwise. You can do this as often as you can afford to pay the cost.

If a Trigger move requires an action roll, resolve it as a normal action roll. Balance and Despair apply — you may clear or mark off further Willpower depending on the outcome.

Opportunity Attack

You can make an opportunity attack when an Adversary leaves Close range, or Near range if your weapon allows. Make an attack roll against the provoking target. The attack resolves before they leave your reach.

Ready Reaction

While in Focus, you prepare a specific action and define the trigger that will cause it to occur. When that trigger happens, you may carry out the readied action. If the action requires a roll, resolve it as normal.

Rituals

Rituals are special actions that unfold over time. You may begin a Ritual when you take the Focus, and some Rituals may continue across multiple turns.

Rituals resolve in one of two ways:

  • Countdown Rituals: A ticking clock that advances with every roll, determined by the GM
  • Target Score Rituals: A running total of progress built up over several rolls

Target Score Rituals

A Target Score Ritual often represents research, repairs, diagnostics, construction, brute-forcing entry, or extended mystical or technological procedures.

  • Tool Proficiency: If proficient in the tool or kit used, add +1d6 to the roll. With expertise, add +2d6. Without the proper tool, you have a −12 penalty.
  • Working Together: At the GM's discretion, multiple Heroes may contribute to the same Ritual, each adding progress when they take the Focus.

Saving Rolls

A saving roll is made to resist danger — toxins, hostile powers, or environmental hazards. You do not choose to make a saving roll. The situation calls for it.

When required, make a saving roll using your Dyad Dice, adding the relevant modifier. Compare the total to the Difficulty. On a success, you resist. On a failure, the effect takes hold.

Strength

Resist being knocked Prone or physically overpowered.

Agility

Avoid an explosion, hazard, or sudden environmental danger.

Force

Resist mental influence, psychic attacks, or Force-based effects.

Resilience

Endure poison, disease, environmental extremes, or physical punishment over time.
