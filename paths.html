Choose a Path

Each Hero follows their Path — the journey that shapes who they become. Your Path unlocks abilities, powers, and an ever-growing die that embodies your mastery. You can branch out into other Paths as you level up, but you cannot change where you began.

How Paths Work

At 1st level, you choose one Path and one Path Archetype.

Your Path provides…
  • Core features like extra skill proficiencies and combat capabilities
  • Access to casting trees for Force, Magick, or Technology abilities
  • Options to expand and refine your powers as you progress
  • A unique Path Die that grows as you level up
Your Archetype provides…
  • Unique combat features and signature abilities
  • Specialised weapon and armour proficiencies
  • Additional casting options or advanced techniques
  • Archetype features at levels 1, 5, and 9

Devout

Core Rules

Using Agility and Force, a Devout inspires allies and strikes with balance, embodying an unwavering belief in the Force in a chaotic galaxy.

Skill Proficiencies: Choose 2 from Athletics, Coordination, Religion, and Stealth

Weapon Proficiencies: Standard physical weapons and standard blasters
Bonus: Increase your minor damage threshold by +1

Devouts allow their unwavering belief in the Force to guide their abilities. Your Focus roll modifier is your Force modifier + proficiency bonus.

Path Archetypes

Cosmic

A vessel for the cosmic will of the Force. By reading its currents, they subtly influence the battlefield and weave the threads of fate.

Guided Actions
1st Level

Add your Mystic Die to any attack roll, ability check, or saving roll you make. If the roll is successful and beats the Difficulty, mark off 1 Willpower.

Cosmic Intervention
5th Level

Once per short or full rest, you can bend fate in a moment of crisis. As a trigger move, when an ally within Near range would be reduced to 0 Hit Point slots, you can mark off 1 Willpower to prevent the damage, leaving them with 1 remaining Hit Point slot.

Cosmic Flow
9th Level

When you or an ally within Far range regains Hit Point slots, you may channel the Force to bolster their recovery:

  • Roll your Mystic Die and add the result to the number of Hit Point slots regained (without exceeding their maximum of 12)
  • If the target is already at maximum, they instead gain temporary Hit Point slots equal to the roll. These absorb damage first and fade after the next combat encounter or 10 minutes

Use this a number of times per full rest equal to your Force modifier (minimum once).

Blademaster

Precision fighters who channel the Force through their lightsabers. They excel in duels, cutting through adversaries with skill and focus while controlling the battlefield through reactive defence.

Duelist
1st Level

Gain access to Block and Parry trigger moves:

  • Block: Block an incoming melee or ranged energy attack. Add Strength or Force modifier to your Evasion score before the GM determines hit or miss.
  • Parry: Reduce melee damage by rolling your Sentinel Die. Surplus damage is deflected back onto the attacker.

If you successfully Parry an attack and reflect surplus damage back, you may immediately make a free melee attack against that target.

Form Mastery
9th Level

When taking the Focus and commencing your turn, select a combat form:

  • Defensive: +2 Evasion until end of your turn

To switch forms mid-Focus, mark off a Willpower.

Striker

Strikers channel the Force directly into their bodies, turning themselves into living weapons.

Living Weapon
1st Level

You channel the Force directly into your strikes, turning your body into a Midichlorian-tipped storm.

  • Use your Force (FOR) modifier in place of Strength (STR) for unarmed strikes and natural weapon attacks
  • Unarmed strikes deal 1d6 + your Force modifier
  • When you hit with an unarmed strike, you may mark off 1 Willpower to either knock the target Prone or impose Disadvantage on their next Attack move
Concussive Wave
5th Level

When you hold the Focus, mark off 1 Willpower and make a casting roll. Compare your result to the Difficulty of each adversary within Near range. On a hit, that adversary takes Sentinel Dice + Force modifier damage and is knocked Prone.

Storm of Echoes
9th Level

When you hold the Focus, mark off 2 Willpower to enter a Storm of Echoes. Until you relinquish the Focus:

  • Your Sentinel Die becomes a d6 for all rolls
  • Damage from your unarmed strikes ignores armour slots
  • The first time each Focus you reduce an adversary to 0 Hit Point slots, you may immediately move up to your speed without making an action roll

1st Level

Transcendence

Overflowing with Power

Bolstered by Magick
1st Level

Grant an allied Hero the use of your Sorcerer Die on their next Dyad Dice roll. You may do this a number of times equal to your Force modifier per short rest.

Choir of the Unseen
5th Level

Mark off 1 Willpower to summon a swirling host of spectral entities in Near range until the end of your next turn. The area becomes lightly obscured by green mist. While the Choir persists, choose one stance:

  • Dirge of Withering: Hostile creatures take minor Force damage when they take the Focus. First attack attempt requires a contested roll or suffers Disadvantage.

  • When a linked vessel takes damage, you may transfer up to half to another linked vessel

You may only have one active bond at a time.

Skeleton Key

Backstab Protocol
5th Level

When you have the Hidden condition and hit an adversary with an attack or casting roll that would deal damage, you may mark off 1 Willpower to: add your Technologist Die to the damage roll and immediately move away from the target without triggering an opportunity attack.

Master of Shadows
9th Level

Double Spike (Passive): Run two Security Spike Rituals simultaneously.

Perfect Disguise (Trigger): When you take the Focus, project a holographic disguise. Gain Advantage on Deception and Negotiation while it holds. The disguise drops the moment you attack, cast a Tech power, or take damage.

Silent Elimination (Trigger): When you hit a target with a melee weapon while undetected, make a casting action roll against their Difficulty. On success, the target gains the Stunned condition until the start of your next turn.

Vanguard

Unfinished

Through Presence and Intelligence, a Vanguard turns a group of individuals into something greater than the sum of their parts.

Skill Proficiencies: Choose 2 from Astrogation, Deception, History, Insight, Medicine, and Negotiation
Saving Roll Proficiencies: Intelligence and Presence
Weapon Proficiencies: Standard blasters and standard physical weapons
Bonus: Increase your minor damage threshold by +1

Assess

Whenever you take the Focus, you may Assess at no cost. Choose one stance — it remains active until you take the Focus again or the encounter ends.

Assess: Target

Designate one visible enemy as the Priority Target. Allies add your PRE modifier to damage rolls against them. The target cannot benefit from the Hidden condition against your allies while you can see it.

Assess: Threat

Identify the most dangerous element in the scene. Allies have advantage on saving rolls against it. Mark off 1 Willpower to delay when it would take the Focus — it loses its next action.

Assess: Weakness

Identify an opening. The next ally to act has advantage on their roll if they exploit it. Mark off 1 Willpower to ask the GM one honest question about the target.

Path Archetypes

Marshal

A battlefield commander who shapes engagements through authority, coordination, and iron-willed precision. Marshals don't wait for the right moment — they create it.

Steel the Line
1st Level
  • When you Assess: Target, allies within Far range who can hear you gain +2 Evasion against the Priority Target until your next Focus
  • When you Assess: Threat, mark off 1 Willpower to immediately clear 1 condition from one ally within Far range
  • When you Assess: Weakness, the GM cannot increase the Difficulty of an allied roll made to exploit the weakness you have identified
Coordinated Strike
5th Level

Once per short rest, when you Assess: Target, choose up to two allies within Far range who can hear you — each may immediately make one attack against the Priority Target as a free trigger move before the Focus moves.

Overwhelming Force
9th Level

You may hold all three Assess stances simultaneously rather than choosing one. Once per full rest, every ally within Far range who can hear you may make one attack against any target as a free trigger move. Enemies do not get trigger moves in response.

Envoy

A master of social force. Negotiation, misdirection, and the kind of quiet authority that makes people question whether they really want to pull that trigger.

Read the Room
1st Level
  • Assess: Target identifies the key decision-maker in any social encounter — allies add your PRE modifier to Influence, Negotiation, and Deception rolls directed at that person
  • Assess: Threat identifies who is most likely to turn the situation hostile. Mark off 1 Willpower to intercept before they act (once per short rest)
  • You never have Disadvantage on Influence, Negotiation, or Deception rolls solely because an NPC's attitude is Hostile
Before It Goes Wrong
5th Level

Once per short rest, when tension is present but no Dyad Dice have been rolled, make an Assess roll against the highest-Difficulty NPC in the scene. Success pauses or de-escalates the threat; failure may make things worse.

Spymaster

An intelligence operative who wins by knowing everything the enemy knows while revealing nothing. Spymasters don't react to situations — they constructed them.

Nothing is Hidden
1st Level

When you use Assess: Weakness's honest question against a named NPC or enemy, you may instead ask: What secret are they keeping? What faction tie aren't they showing? What vulnerability do they have?

When an NPC attempts to use Influence against you, they must succeed on a contested roll against your Assess modifier or automatically fail.

I Have Friends Everywhere
5th Level

Once per short rest, describe false or selectively true information you have arranged to reach a specific NPC or faction. The GM must have them act on it as true — until they have reason to doubt it.

Distraction Tactics

Progressing Your Path

Heroes advance when they reach milestones set by the GM — defeating key adversaries, completing objectives, or advancing the story. Each milestone grants a new level.

When You Gain a Level

  1. Select Path Progression Options

    Select two options from your Path Progression list. You may choose from your current tier or any previous tier.

  2. Adjust Your Stats

    Apply any increases or modifications. Record these in the relevant rows of your Auxiliary tables.

  3. Gain a New Card

    At levels 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8, 10, 11, and 12, select one new card from your Path or Path Archetype.

  4. Gain an Archetype Feature

    At levels 5 and 9, gain an additional feature from your Path Archetype.

Diversifying Your Path

Expand within your current Path by accessing an additional Archetype.

  • Select Diversify Path as a Path Progression option (costs 2 selections, can span separate levels)
  • You gain access to cards from a second Archetype within the same Path
  • You do not gain that Archetype's features as you level — only your primary Archetype grants features

Multi-Path

Branch into entirely new Paths, reflecting growth, change, or narrative direction.

  • Select Multi-Path as a Path Progression option (costs 2 selections)
  • Choose a new Path and one of its Archetypes
  • You gain access to that Path's cards and progression options
  • You may need to meet Trait modifier prerequisites
  • You do not gain all features of that Path as if it were your starting Path
  • Multi-Pathing provides breadth, but at a reduced depth compared to your primary Path
