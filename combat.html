Combat

When the blasters come out and the lightsabers ignite, the galaxy hits back. Here's how attacks, damage, conditions, and death play out at the table.

Making an Attack

When you take the Attack action, you make an action roll against a target to inflict harm.

Ranged Attacks

Target an enemy at a distance — firing a blaster, throwing a weapon, or projecting a power. Use your Agility (AGI) trait modifier as the attack bonus.

Ranged weapons have an optimal range:

  • Disadvantage if the target is beyond optimal range
  • Disadvantage if the target is within Close range
  • Automatic miss if the target is more than one range step beyond the weapon's optimal range
Melee Attacks

Target an enemy within Close range. Use your Strength (STR) trait modifier as the attack bonus. These attacks use hand-held weapons, improvised objects, unarmed strikes, or natural weapons.

Attacking Against the Unseen

  • If you attack a target you cannot see, roll with Disadvantage. If the target is not in the location you chose, the attack misses.
  • If a target cannot see you, your attacks against it have Advantage
  • If you are hidden when you attack, your position is revealed after the attack resolves

💚
Healing

Healing isn't damage — but it works the same way. Healing rolls follow the same dice and threshold rules as damage rolls. Instead of marking Hit Point slots, you clear them.

Resistances, Immunities & Vulnerabilities

Resistant

Damage of that type is halved before you compare it to your Hit Point thresholds. Apply resistance first, then spend armour slots. Multiple resistances to the same damage type don't stack.

Immune

Damage of that type is ignored entirely. No threshold check, no slots marked, no roll to make.

Vulnerable

Damage of that type is doubled before you compare it to your Hit Point thresholds.

Mixed Damage

Some attacks deal more than one type at once — a flaming vibroblade might deal Physical and Fire. You only benefit from resistance or immunity if you have it against every damage type in the attack. Vulnerability to any type in the mix still applies, and the full damage is doubled before thresholds are checked.

Armour & Damage

When you take damage, you may expend 1 armour slot to reduce the damage threshold by one step (e.g., Severe → Major). You cannot expend multiple slots at once — only one slot may be used each time you take damage.

💀
Instant Death

If a Hero takes damage equal to twice their Massive damage threshold in a single hit, they die instantly.

Armour Slots

  • When all slots are filled, the armour is broken — it must be repaired during downtime before it can be used again
  • Equipping new armour refreshes your armour slots to the new maximum; the old armour is spent until repaired
  • Being unarmoured provides no bonus or penalty — you cannot use armour slots unless you have an armour score
  • If a power or ability grants a temporary armour score, you also gain extra slots equal to the temporary score. When the effect ends, those slots are removed

Movement

When you make an action roll, you may also move within Close range as part of that action. If you want to move beyond Close range, or to a location that is difficult to access, make an Athletics (STR) roll to reposition. The GM sets the Difficulty based on the situation.

Cover & the Dyad Dice

Combat isn't static. Cover shifts and breaks under fire. A Balance result means cover holds firm. A Despair result may cause cover to splinter, collapse, or fail at the GM's discretion — a crate explodes, a wall begins to crumble. The GM may spend Despair tokens to turn cover into a complication or danger.

Conditions

Conditions represent persistent mechanical states affecting a Hero, adversary, or NPC.

Dying
Duration: Until recovered | Recovery: Restore at least 1 HP slot through healing or an effect

Once all Hit Point slots are depleted, you gain the Dying condition. You fall Prone, relying solely on your Willpower to survive.

  • All Willpower costs and penalties are doubled
  • If you take the Focus while Dying, speed is halved (a crawl unless you stand) and all action rolls are made with Disadvantage
  • If you take damage while Dying, mark Willpower slots instead of HP slots (you may still use armour)
Frightened
Duration: Until the source of fear is out of sight | Recovery: Successful Presence Saving Roll when you next take the Focus

Terror grips your spirit. You have Disadvantage on Dyad Dice rolls while the source of your fear is within sight, and you cannot willingly move closer to it.

Hidden
Duration: While unseen | Recovery: Making a loud or visible move, attacking, or being discovered

You're concealed from sight and detection. You cannot be directly targeted by attacks or abilities until revealed. Attacks made from hiding have Advantage.

Ignited
Duration: Persists until extinguished | Recovery: Mark off 1 Willpower to submerge or douse yourself

Flames engulf you. Each time you take the Focus, take 1d6 FIR damage.

Influenced
Duration: As stated by the influencing effect | Recovery: When the influence ends or is dispelled

Your will is not your own. You regard the influencer as an ally and will not willingly harm them. Depending on the power or ability, you may follow simple commands or act in their interest.

Immobilised
Duration: Countdown 6 or until freed | Recovery: You or an ally mark off a Willpower to release you

You are locked in place. Your speed becomes 0, and you cannot benefit from movement bonuses until the effect ends.

Ionised
Droids and cybernetic beings only. Duration: Countdown 6 or after a short rest | Recovery: Mark off 1 Willpower to remove

You're disrupted by electrical feedback. You are either Stunned or Weakened, depending on the severity of the ion surge.

Prone
Duration: Until you stand | Recovery: Use the Stand Up manoeuvre

You've hit the ground. Melee attacks against you have Advantage. Ranged attacks against you have Disadvantage. Your own attacks are made with Disadvantage.

Stunned
Duration: Countdown 6 or as specified | Recovery: Ends automatically or when you receive aid

Your body seizes and your mind reels. You cannot take the Focus until the condition ends.

Unconscious
Duration: Until short or full rest and recovering at least 1 Willpower slot

You fall still and silent. You drop anything held, fall Prone, and cannot take the Focus. Attack rolls against you have Advantage. Melee hits are treated as Minor Critical hits.

Weakened
Duration: Countdown 6 or after a short rest | Recovery: Short rest or through aid

Your strength falters and precision slips. You have Disadvantage on all action rolls and your speed is halved.

Death

Death happens when all Hit Points and all Willpower slots are depleted. The character collapses and cannot take further actions except death moves.

☯️
Death Moves

When you die, you may take one of three death moves. One allows you to cling onto life at a cost. Another allows you to go out in a blaze of glory. The third puts your fate in the hands of your dice.

Cling to Life

You fall unconscious until it is safe to be revived (usually after combat). Once revived, permanently mark off your highest 3 Willpower slots, reducing your max Willpower capacity.

If all Willpower slots are permanently marked off, you can no longer cling to life. You die and become one with the Force.

Blaze of Glory

You may take one final heroic action at no cost. This action automatically counts as a Major Critical Success. Afterward, you have a moment to say farewell before becoming one with the Force.

Trust in the Force

Roll your Dyad Dice:

  • If the Balance die is higher: your character clears all HP and WP slots
  • If the Despair die is higher: your character crosses through the veil and becomes one with the Force
  • If the dice show matching results: your character returns to their feet, all HP and WP cleared

If your character dies, work with the GM before the next session to create a new character at the current level of the rest of the party.

Returning from Death

Revive

A character that has been dead for a short duration may be revived by a power or ability that specifically states the time limit of death it can affect. Only abilities, Force powers, or technologies that explicitly mention revival after death can bring a character back.

Resurrect

Resurrection requires significant effort and often great cost. When resurrected, permanently mark off 6 Willpower slots (−6 penalty to all rolls). This stacks each time you are resurrected.

Once all Willpower slots are permanently marked off, you cannot be resurrected again and die permanently. The GM may allow opportunities to reclaim permanently marked off Willpower slots.

Consciousness Transfer

Lifeforms may transfer essence into another body, or droids may restore a data backup.

  • Essence Transfer: The host may resist, causing issues or failure. A Difficulty Challenge may also be required.
  • Data Bank Backup (Droids): Data banks may be corrupted, causing patchy or aberrant memories. Unwilling recipients may resist backup installation.
